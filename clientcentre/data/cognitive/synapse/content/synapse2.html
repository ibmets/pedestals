The True North chip contains one million individual programmable neurons and consumes 70mW total power while running a typical recurrent network at biological real-time, four orders of magnitude lower than a conventional computer running the same network.

The parallel, flexible and scalable architecture has been successfully put to work on a number of cognitive applications including computer vision and has the potential to revolutionize the computer industry by integrating brain-like capability into devices where computation is constrained by power and speed.
